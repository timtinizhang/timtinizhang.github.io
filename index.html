Development is the hard truth.

I am a final-year undergraduate student majoring in International and Public Affairs at the University of Macau, where I received a full scholarship. From 2025 to 2027, I also serve as the Head of Academics of the Youth Association of International Affairs.

My research interests lie in development economics, political economy, labor economics, and international economics. I study how globalization, domestic institutions, and development strategies shape production, distribution, and socioeconomic pressures in China and East Asia, as well as how central and local governments make policy choices under institutional, market, legal, and political constraints.

I have completed extensive training in quantitative and empirical methods with excellent academic performance, alongside long-term engagement and substantial hands-on experience in political science and political economy. This background has equipped me with a solid foundation in literature review, research design, and data analysis, with methodological strengths in econometric and statistical methods, formal theory, R programming, computational text analysis, survey experiments, and social network analysis.
