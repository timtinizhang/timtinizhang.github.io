Research

Statement of Interest

My primary research interest asks how open economy, domestic institutions, and development strategies interact to reshape production structures and distributional outcomes in China and East Asia, and how these transformations further generate socioeconomic tensions across periods of growth and slowdown, especially through their consequences for beliefs and attitudes, labor-market dynamics, and education.

In practice, one of my ongoing projects uses a survey experiment to examine how exposure to foreign anti-elite rhetoric affects beliefs about inequality, labor-market conditions, and social mobility in China, and whether these effects vary with individuals’ economic insecurity. This project connects cross-border information flows to broader questions about development, distribution, and socioeconomic pressure.

A secondary line of my research analyzes how central and local officials navigate complex institutional and political-economic constraints to formulate economic policies and make political choices. This is particularly connected to issues of urbanization, infrastructure, and regional development.
