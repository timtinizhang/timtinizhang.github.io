Hi! You can call me Tim.

I am a final-year undergraduate student majoring in International and Public Affairs at the University of Macau, where I received a full scholarship. From 2025 to 2027, I also serve as the Head of Academics of the Youth Association of International Affairs.

My research interests lie at the intersection of Comparative Political Economy, International Political Economy, and the Political Economy of Development, with a regional focus on China and East Asia. I explore how international politics and the open economy shape domestic modes of production and development strategies, connecting these to issues such as public opinion, inequality, labor, and education; as well as how complex domestic and international political-economic factors drive government responsiveness, governance, and foreign policy.

My extensive engagement and hands-on experience in political science research have equipped me with solid skills in literature review, research design, and data analysis, with a particular emphasis on methods such as computational text analysis, statistical inference, survey experiments, and social network analysis. In my independent projects, I also primarily employ quantitative and computational tools to conduct empirical research.

Outside academia, I am a geography enthusiast with a broad interest in maps, cities, and public transportation.
